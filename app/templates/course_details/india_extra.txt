h3. Bhagavad-Gita

Bhagavadgita in Sanskrit meaning “Song of God” is a section in the Mahabharata which is one of the oldest and the longest texts of Indian philosophy/literature which is said to contain in myth form many complex scientific and mathematical ideas. The Bhagavad-Gita has been a cherished source of spiritual guidance for many centuries and despite being more than 2000 years old, it contains many teachings relevant for human beings and the crisis we find in our own times. In this part of the course you will be introduced to the key characters of the story, such as, Krishna and Arjuna, also the two families, the Pandavas and Kuravas and will reflect upon how the events described represent the universal inner conflict within the human being. By exploring the symbolism of the story as well as the characters, we will try to achieve a better understanding of the sevenfold constitution of the human being and the latent potential within each individual.

h3. Karma, Dharma and Reincarnation

The theory of karma, dharma and reincarnation are fundamental ideas in eastern philosophy. In this part of the course, we will explore what do these concepts really mean and ask what are their philosophical and practical implications? 
This will shed light onto deeper questions such as ‘does free will exist?’.
